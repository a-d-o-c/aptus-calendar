What is Aptus?

Aptus (Latin: aligned, fitted, suited to the time) is a calendar system designed for seasonal coherence rather than historical accident.

The Gregorian calendar works for coordination, but it's poorly aligned with observable reality. Months are uneven, the year begins arbitrarily, and for the Southern Hemisphere, the seasonal labels are backwards.

Aptus addresses this through structure:

  • 13 months × 28 days = 364 days, every month identical
  • Lacuna: A threshold day outside the structure, between years
  • Equinox anchor: Year begins at spring (Sept 22 SH, Mar 20 NH)
  • Weekly cadence: Every month follows the same four-phase cycle

This is a temporal operating system, not a doctrine. Use it if it helps.

How It Works

01

Hemisphere-Aware

Southern Hemisphere anchors to Sept 22. Northern to March 20. Seasons align with actual sunlight, not administrative convention.

02

13 Equal Months

Every month is exactly 28 days — four perfect weeks. No confusion about how many days remain. Every month behaves identically.

03

Weekly Cadence

Each month follows the same four phases: Orient → Engage → Amplify → Integrate. A repeating production cycle you can internalise.

04

Lacuna & Aequa

Day 365 is Lacuna — outside the structure, for threshold work. Aequa is a rare calibration day (~every 6 years) for solar precision.
