What is Aptus?

Aptus (Latin: aligned, fitted, suited to the time) is a calendar system designed for seasonal coherence rather than historical accident.

The Gregorian calendar works for coordination, but it's poorly aligned with observable reality. Months are uneven (28 to 31 days), the year begins arbitrarily (January has no seasonal significance), and for the Southern Hemisphere, the seasonal labels are backwards.

Aptus addresses this through structure:

  • 13 months × 28 days = 364 days
  • Lacuna: A threshold day between years
  • Equinox anchor: Year begins at spring (Sept 22 SH, Mar 20 NH)
  • Seasonal alignment: Month names and focuses match natural cycles

This is a temporal operating system, not a doctrine. Use it if it helps.

How It Works

🌍

Hemisphere-Aware

🔄

Weekly Cadence

Each month follows: Orient → Engage → Amplify → Integrate. Repeating production cycle.

🌙

Lacuna

Day 365 sits outside the structure. A threshold for reset, review, and intention-setting before the new year.
