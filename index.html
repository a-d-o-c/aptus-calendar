Thirteen Months

Each month is 28 days — four identical weeks. Every month has a name, a season, and a focus that describes its energetic quality.

What is Aptus?

Aptus (Latin: aligned, fitted, suited to the time) is a calendar system designed for seasonal coherence rather than historical accident.

The Gregorian calendar works for coordination, but it's poorly aligned with observable reality. Months are uneven, the year begins arbitrarily, and for the Southern Hemisphere, the seasonal labels are backwards.

Aptus addresses this: 13 months × 28 days = 364 days, every month identical. The year begins at spring equinox (Sept 22 SH). Day 365 is Lacuna — a threshold day outside the structure. Every month follows the same four-phase weekly cycle: Orient → Engage → Amplify → Integrate.

This is a temporal operating system, not a doctrine. Use it if it helps.
